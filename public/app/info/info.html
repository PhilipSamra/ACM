• What is ACM?
    ACM, or the Association for Computing Machinery, is the world's oldest and largest educational and scientific computing society. ACM is a leading resource for computing professionals and students working in the various fields of Information Technology, and for interpreting the impact of information technology on society. The Catholic University of America ACM chapter strives to promote a greater interest in computing and its applications and promote a means of communication between persons with an interest in computer science.

  • Who is eligible to participate?
    ACM is for anyone who has an interest in computer science, regardless of major or background.

  • What are the requirements?
    Local chapter membership is free and open to everyone. You are invited to attend meetings as well as participate in any scheduled events. National ACM membership provides access to online books, courses, and publications for a small annual fee.

  • How do I join?
    We are always looking for new members, so if you are interested in learning more about the CUA ACM local chapter contact any E-board members, or faculty advisors.
